Orbital Machine Polishing: A Beginner's Guide

Master polishing with an orbital machine. Choice of pads, compounds, movement techniques and how to avoid holograms.

Why the Orbital Machine is Ideal for Beginners

The orbital polisher (also called DA - Dual Action) is the safest tool for those starting out in the world of automotive polishing. Unlike rotary polishers, it has an orbital movement that drastically reduces the risk of burning the paintwork.

Advantages of the orbital:

  • Virtually impossible to burn the paint
  • No visible holograms
  • Easier to control
  • Professional results with practice
  • Versatile for various stages

Understanding Paint Defects

Types of defects

  • Swirl marks: Circular micro-scratches (caused by incorrect washing)
  • Light scratches: Visible under direct light
  • Deep scratches: You can feel them with your fingernail
  • Oxidation: Dull, opaque paintwork
  • Holograms: Previous polishing marks
  • Water spots: Mineral water stains

üí° Nail test

Run your fingernail lightly over the scratch. If the nail "sticks", the scratch is too deep for polishing. Scratches that you feel with your fingernail have usually reached the primer and need a paint touch-up.

Polishing pads

Pads are classified by color and hardness:

  • Yellow/Orange (Cutting): More aggressive, for compounding
  • White/Green (Polishing): Medium, for polish
  • Black/Blue (Finishing): Soft, for final finishing

Recommended brands: Meguiar's, Lake Country, Buff and Shine, 3M

Compounds and Polishes

  • Compound (Cutting): Removes deep defects
  • Polish: Removes holograms and adds shine
  • Finishing Polish: Final mirror finish

Preparation: The Basis for Success

Step 1: Thorough washing

  1. Wash with the two bucket method
  2. Use clay bar to decontaminate
  3. Dry completely
  4. Work in a covered, well-lit area

Step 2: Inspection

  • Use a strong LED flashlight
  • Inspect each panel under direct light
  • Mark problem areas with masking tape
  • Photograph defects to compare later

Step 3: Test Polish

Choose a small, inconspicuous area (e.g. bottom of door) and test your pad + compound combination. This avoids unpleasant surprises.

Polishing Technique Step by Step

Initial setup

  1. Install the appropriate pad on the polisher
  2. Apply 4-5 dots of compound to the pad
  3. Spread the product with the machine switched off
  4. Divide the panel into 40x40cm sections

Polishing process

  1. Initial speed: Start at speed 2-3
  2. Pressure: Let the weight of the machine do the work
  3. Movement: Slow horizontal passes (50% overlap)
  4. After: Vertical passes in the same area
  5. Repeat: 4-6 passes in each direction
  6. Reduce speed: Last 2 passes at speed 1
  7. Clean: Remove residue with microfiber towel
  8. Inspect: Check result under light

Movement pattern

50% overlap technique:

  • Each pass should overlap 50% of the previous one
  • Constant, slow movement (5-10 cm/second)
  • Never stop the machine on the paint
  • Always keep the pad flat on the surface

3-stage system

Step 1: Cutting

  • Pad: Yellow/Orange (cutting)
  • Product: Aggressive compound
  • Speed: 4-5
  • Objective: Remove deep defects
  • Passes: 6-8 in each direction

Stage 2: Polishing

  • Pad: White/Green (polishing)
  • Product: Medium Polish
  • Speed: 4-5
  • Objective: To remove holograms from the compound
  • Passes: 4-6 in each direction

Step 3: Finishing

  • Pad: Black/Blue (finishing)
  • Product: Finishing polish
  • Speed: 3-4
  • Objective: Final mirror shine
  • Passes: 3-4 in each direction

üí° Pro Tip

You don't always need all 3 steps. For light defects, start straight at the polish (step 2). For paintwork in good condition, just finishing may be enough.

Care for Sensitive Areas

Risk areas

  • Corners and edges: Thinner paint, easy to burn off
  • Plastics: Protect with masking tape
  • Rubbers: Compound stains permanently
  • Emblems: Cover or remove if possible

Technique for corners

  • Use smaller pad (3" or 4")
  • Reduce speed to 2-3
  • Minimum pressure
  • Fewer passes (2-3 only)
  • Or simply avoid - corners are not critical

Pad maintenance

During Work

  • Clean pad every 2-3 sections
  • Use soft bristle brush
  • Remove accumulated residue
  • Saturated pad won't cut

After use

  1. Wash with warm water and mild soap
  2. Rub gently to remove product
  3. Rinse thoroughly
  4. Leave to dry naturally
  5. Store in a dry place

Common beginner's mistakes

  1. Too much pressure: Let the machine work on its own
  2. Speed too high: Start at 4-5, not 6
  3. Moving too fast: Slow and steady wins
  4. Too much product: 4-5 stitches are enough
  5. Dirty pad: Clean frequently
  6. Don't test first: Always test in a small area
  7. Working in the sun: Product dries too quickly
  8. Skipping steps: Compound without polish leaves holograms

How long does it take?

First polish (learning):

  • Small car: 6-8 hours
  • Medium car: 8-12 hours
  • SUV/Pickup: 12-16 hours

With experience:

  • Small car: 3-4 hours
  • Medium car: 4-6 hours
  • SUV/Pickup: 6-8 hours

Conclusion

Polishing with an orbital machine is a skill that can be learned with practice. Your first job won't be perfect, and that's okay. The important thing is to start slowly, always test and take your time.

Final tips:

  • Start in an old car or a friend's car that you don't mind
  • Watch professional videos on YouTube
  • Invest in good products - they make a real difference
  • Don't be discouraged by early results
  • Practice makes perfect

With patience and the right technique, you'll achieve results to rival the professionals!
