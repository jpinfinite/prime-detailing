How to Apply Ceramic Coating: Complete Step-by-Step

Detailed tutorial on applying ceramic tiles. Surface preparation, application techniques and post-application care.

What is Ceramic Coating?

Ceramic coating is a liquid protective layer based on nanotechnology which, when applied to paint, creates a permanent chemical bond with the surface. Unlike waxes and sealants, it is not removed by washing.

Main benefits:

  • Protection against UV rays, oxidation and chemical contaminants
  • Extreme hydrophobic effect (water runs off easily)
  • Lasts from 2 to 5 years (depending on the product)
  • Easy cleaning and maintenance
  • Deep, long-lasting shine
  • Resistance to light scratches

Complete Step-by-Step

Step 1: Deep Wash (1-2 hours)

  1. Wash the car using the two bucket method
  2. Apply ferrous decontaminant to all paintwork
  3. Wait 5 minutes (it will turn purple where there is contamination)
  4. Rinse thoroughly
  5. Use clay bar on entire surface to remove contaminants
  6. Rinse again and dry completely

üí° Plastic bag test

Run a plastic bag over the paint. If it feels rough, there are still contaminants. Continue with the clay bar until it is as smooth as glass.

Stage 2: Paint Correction (2-4 hours)

This is the most important and labor-intensive stage:

  1. Inspection: Use strong light to identify defects
  2. Compound: Remove deep scratches with aggressive compound
  3. Polish: Refine with fine polish to remove holograms
  4. Check: Inspect under direct light

Tip: If you don't have experience with polishing, consider hiring a professional for this step. A poorly applied coating can be removed, but a burnt paint job has no comeback.

Step 3: Final preparation (30 minutes)

  1. Clean all paintwork with IPA diluted 1:1
  2. Use a clean microfiber towel
  3. This removes oils, waxes and polish residue
  4. Leave to dry completely (10-15 minutes)
  5. Protect rubbers, plastics and glass with masking tape

Step 4: Apply Ceramic Coating (1-2 hours)

‚ö†Ô∏è CRUCIAL: Work panel by panel

NEVER apply to the whole car at once. The coating cures quickly and can stain if you don't remove it in time.

Application technique:

  1. Place 3-5 drops of the coating on the applicator pad
  2. Apply in criss-cross movements (vertical + horizontal)
  3. Cover a small panel (e.g. half the hood)
  4. Wait for the "flash time" (30 seconds to 2 minutes - see instructions)
  5. The coating will start to "rainbow" when it's ready
  6. Remove with microfiber towel in straight movements
  7. Make a second pass with a clean towel to make sure
  8. Move on to next panel

Recommended order of application

  1. Roof
  2. Hood (divide into 2-3 sections)
  3. Front fenders
  4. Doors (one at a time)
  5. Rear fenders
  6. Rear cover
  7. Bumpers

Step 5: Initial cure (12-24 hours)

  • Leave the car in a covered, dry place
  • DO NOT get wet for 24 hours
  • DO NOT touch the paintwork
  • Avoid dust and contaminants

Fatal Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Applying to dirty/contaminated paint - will seal in dirt
  2. Don't do paint correction - It will seal scratches
  3. Apply to too many panels at once - Will stain
  4. Don't remove at the right time - Permanent stains
  5. Apply in sun or heat - Cures too quickly
  6. Use the wrong products during maintenance - Degrades the coating
  7. Wetting before 24 hours - Compromises durability

Is it worth doing it yourself?

Do it yourself if:

  • You have experience with detailing
  • You have the right tools (polisher, etc.)
  • You have a suitable location (closed garage)
  • You have the time (at least 6-8 hours)
  • You want to save money (professional application costs R$2,000-5,000)

Hire a professional if

  • You've never done paint correction before
  • You have no tools
  • Paint has severe defects
  • You want a guaranteed result
  • You don't want to take any risks

Conclusion

Ceramic coating is the best investment in automotive protection, but it requires meticulous preparation. The difference between a professional result and a disaster is in the details.

If you decide to do it yourself, take your time. It's better to take 2 days to do it right than to have to remove and redo it.

Remember: The preparation is more important than the product. A well applied cheap coating outweighs a badly applied expensive coating.
