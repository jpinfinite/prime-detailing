Wheel and Tire Detailing: Essential Products

The best products for wheel cleaning, rust removal, tire protection and professional finishing.

Why wheels and tires deserve special attention

Wheels and tires are the dirtiest parts of the car, exposed to:

  • Brake dust (extremely corrosive)
  • Road dirt (tar, oil)
  • Salt and chemicals (winter)
  • Extreme heat from the brakes
  • Ferrous contamination

Unsuitable products can:

  • ❌ Damage wheel finish
  • dry out and crack tires
  • ❌ Cause corrosion
  • ❌ Stain permanently

Complete Cleaning Process

Step 1: Pre-wash (5 minutes)

  1. Rinse wheels with high-pressure water
  2. Remove large debris
  3. Wet thoroughly

Step 2: Product Application (10 minutes)

  1. Apply wheel cleaner generously
  2. Start with the dirtiest wheels
  3. Wait recommended time (1-5 min)
  4. DO NOT allow to dry

Step 3: Agitation (10 minutes)

  1. Use wheel brush on spokes
  2. Detail brush on screws and logos
  3. Woolies on the barrel (inside)
  4. Tire brush on the sides

Step 4: Rinsing (5 minutes)

  1. Rinse thoroughly with high pressure
  2. Make sure to remove all product
  3. Rinse from top to bottom

Step 5: Drying (5 minutes)

  1. Dry wheels with microfiber towel
  2. Dry tires (important for dressing)
  3. Use compressed air on details (optional)

Step 6: Protection (10 minutes)

  1. Apply sealant or wax to wheels (optional)
  2. Apply tire dressing to tires
  3. Let cure for 10-15 minutes
  4. Remove excess if necessary

Care by Wheel Type

Painted wheels (most common)

  • you can use any wheel cleaner
  • ✅ Soft bristle brushes
  • ✅ Sealant or wax recommended
  • ⚠️ Beware of very acidic products

Chrome wheels

  • ✅ Use pH neutral products
  • very soft brushes
  • ✅ Dry immediately (water stains)
  • avoid strong acid products

Polished wheels/Aluminum

  • ✅ Neutral pH required
  • ✅ Specific polish for aluminum
  • ✅ Sealant is essential
  • ❌ Never use acid

Anodized wheels

  • ✅ Neutral pH only
  • ✅ Extreme care (delicate finish)
  • ❌ Acids destroy anodizing

Black/graphite wheels

  • ✅ Any wheel cleaner
  • ✅ Tire dressing can be used on wheels too
  • ✅ More tolerant to products

Common mistakes to avoid

  1. Letting wheel cleaner dry: Can stain permanently
  2. Using the same glove as the paint: Contaminates and scratches
  3. Don't rinse thoroughly: Residue causes stains
  4. Applying tire dressing to a dirty tire: Sealsin dirt
  5. Tire dressing on the tread: Dangerous, slips off
  6. Wrong product for wheel type: Can damage
  7. Too hard brushes: Scratches finish
  8. Do not dry before dressing: Does not adhere well
