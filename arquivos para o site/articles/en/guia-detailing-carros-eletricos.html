Detailing in Electric Cars: Special Care

A specialized guide to detailing electric vehicles. Differences, precautions and specific techniques for EVs.

Why are EVs different?

Electric cars have particularities that require special care when detailing. It's not just "washing a car without an engine" - there are important differences in materials, components and sensitive areas.

Main differences:

  • Water-sensitive charging port
  • High-voltage batteries and electrical components
  • Sustainable materials (vegan leather, recycled fabrics)
  • More sensitive screens and electronics
  • No traditional engine (frunk)
  • Regenerative brakes (less brake dust)

Areas of Special Attention

1. Charging port

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: Never Wet Directly

The charging port is the most sensitive area of an EV. Water at high pressure can damage connectors and cause short-circuiting.

How to clean properly:

  • Keep door CLOSED during washing
  • Never use a high-pressure jet directly on the port
  • If you need to clean internally: use a damp cloth (not wet)
  • Dry completely before charging
  • Apply rubber protector to seals

2. Frunk (Front Compartment)

Where the engine would be, EVs have a charging compartment:

  • May have sensitive electronic components
  • Avoid excessive wetting
  • Use a vacuum cleaner and damp cloth
  • Check water drains

3. Underbody

Battery is under the car:

  • Robust protection, but avoid direct jets on connectors
  • Beware of high-pressure chassis washing
  • Do not use strong acid products
  • Inspect regularly for damage

External washing of EVs

Preparation

  1. Check that the charging port is CLOSED
  2. Close all windows completely
  3. Turn off the car (not just "park")
  4. Check that sentry mode is switched off (Tesla)

Washing process

The process is 95% the same as conventional cars:

  1. Pre-wash with foam cannon
  2. Two bucket method
  3. Avoid direct spray on the charging port
  4. Special attention to wheels (less brake dust)
  5. Thorough rinsing
  6. Drying with microfiber towels

No combustion engine = no oil, no soot. The front end is much cleaner!

EV Internal Detailing

Screens and electronics

EVs have many more screens than conventional cars:

  • Never use: Ammonia-based products, strong alcohol, acetone
  • Use: Specific screen cleaner or distilled water
  • Technique: Slightly damp microfiber cloth, gentle movements
  • Drying: Dry cloth immediately after

Sustainable materials

Many EVs use vegan/recycled materials:

Vegan leather (Tesla, Polestar, etc.)

  • More stain-resistant than real leather
  • Clean with APC diluted 1:10
  • Doesn't need moisturizing like real leather
  • UV protector is recommended

Recycled fabrics (Volvo, BMW i)

  • Vacuum regularly
  • Steam extraction works well
  • Avoid very oily products
  • Dry thoroughly to avoid mold

Glass and Panoramic Roof

Many EVs have large glass ceilings:

  • Clean inside and out
  • Use ammonia-free glass cleaner
  • Consider UV protection film
  • Ceramic coating on the glass helps a lot

Polishing and Paint Correction

Painting EVs

Generally no significant difference, but:

  • Tesla: Notoriously thin and soft paint (easy to scratch)
  • Porsche Taycan: Hard and resistant paint
  • BMW i: Standard BMW paint (good quality)
  • BYD: Soft paint, beware of aggressive compounding

Special care

  • Measure thickness before polishing (especially Tesla)
  • Start with less aggressive products
  • Protect sensors and cameras with tape
  • Watch out for emblems (some are adhesive)

Recommended Protection for EVs

Why do EVs need more protection?

  • They are usually new and expensive cars
  • Owners want to preserve value
  • Paintwork can be more delicate
  • Less maintenance = more time between cleanings

Protection Options

1. Ceramic Coating (Most Popular)

  • Ideal for EVs as it makes cleaning easier
  • Hydrophobicity reduces water consumption
  • UV protection important for glass roofs
  • Cost: R$ 2.000-4.000

2. PPF (Maximum Protection)

  • Recommended for Tesla (delicate paintwork)
  • Protects against road stones
  • Preserves resale value
  • Cost: R$8,000-20,000 (complete)

3. Hybrid (best value for money)

  • PPF on hood, bumper, mirrors
  • Ceramic coating on the rest
  • Cost: R$ 4,000-8,000

Brand-specific care

Tesla (Model 3, Y, S, X)

  • Paint: Very thin, scratches easily
  • Screens: Clean with damp cloth only
  • Sentry mode: Turn off before washing
  • Recommendation: PPF on hood at least

BYD (Dolphin, Seal, Tan)

  • Paint: Smooth, take care with polishing
  • Interior: Good quality materials
  • Cargo door: Good seal, but take care

Porsche Taycan

  • Paint: Excellent quality, hard wearing
  • Interior: Premium leather, use specific products
  • Wheels: Frequent cleaning (brake dust still exists)

BMW i4/iX

  • Paint: BMW standard (good)
  • Grille: Not functional, just aesthetic
  • Interior: Sustainable materials, special care

Myths about Detailing EVs

mYTH: "You can't wash EVs in the rain"

TRUTH: EVs are completely waterproof. You can wash them normally, even in the rain.

mYTH: "You can't use a pressure washer"

TRUTH: You can use it, just avoid direct spray on the charging port.

mYTH: "You need special products"

TRUTH: 95% of normal products work. Just be careful with screens and vegan materials.

‚úÖ TRUTH: "EVs stay cleaner for longer"

Yes, without a combustion engine, there's no soot and oil. The hood/frunk stays much cleaner.

Conclusion

Detailing electric cars isn't complicated - it's 95% the same as conventional cars. The main differences are:

  • Care with the charging port
  • Attention to sustainable materials in the interior
  • More screens = more care with products
  • Paintwork can be more delicate (especially Tesla)

With these simple precautions, you can do professional detailing on any EV safely and with excellent results!
