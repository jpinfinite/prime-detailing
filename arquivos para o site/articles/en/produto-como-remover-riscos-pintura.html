Manual Removal Technique

Materials needed

  • Scratch remover
  • Foam or microfiber applicator
  • 3-4 clean microfiber towels
  • Masking tape (to demarcate the area)
  • Water and shampoo (for pre-cleaning)

Step by step

  1. Cleaning: Wash and dry the area thoroughly
  2. Delimitation: Use masking tape around the scratch
  3. Application: Pour product into the applicator
  4. Work: Circular movements with medium pressure
  5. Time: 2-3 minutes per small area
  6. Removal: Wipe with microfiber towel
  7. Inspection: Check under bright light
  8. Repeat: If necessary, repeat process

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Cautions

  • Do not work in direct sunlight
  • Do not allow product to dry
  • Do not use too much pressure
  • Work on a small area at a time

Orbital Machine Technique

When to use the machine

  • Medium scratches that are not in the manual
  • Multiple scratches in a large area
  • You want a professional result
  • Have experience with a polisher

Settings

  • Pad: Orange (cutting) for compound
  • Product: Aggressive compound
  • Speed: 4-5 (orbital)
  • Pressure: Medium

Process

  1. Apply 4-5 dots of compound to the pad
  2. Spread with machine switched off
  3. Turn on low speed (2)
  4. Increase to 4-5
  5. Slow passes over the scratch
  6. 6-8 passes in each direction
  7. Clean and inspect
  8. Polish if necessary

Touching up deep scratches

Preparation

  1. Clean area thoroughly
  2. Sand lightly with 2000 (if necessary)
  3. Wipe with isopropyl alcohol
  4. Let dry completely

Paint application

  1. Shake pen/bottle for 2 minutes
  2. Apply VERY thin layer
  3. Let dry for 15-20 minutes
  4. Apply second coat
  5. Repeat until even with paint
  6. Let cure 24-48 hours

Leveling

  1. Sand with 2000 wet (very carefully)
  2. Polish with light compound
  3. Refine with fine polish
  4. Apply wax or sealant

Risk prevention

Correct washing

  • Two bucket method
  • Microfiber gloves (not sponges)
  • Straight movements (not circular)
  • Pre-wash with foam cannon

Protection

  • Ceramic coating or regular wax
  • PPF in high-impact areas
  • Park away from other cars
  • Avoid automatic car washes

Daily care

  • Do not rest objects on the paintwork
  • Be careful with backpacks/bags
  • Do not dry dust
  • Use a cover when parking on the street

Myths about scratch removal

mYTH: "Toothpaste removes scratches"

TRUTH: It can mask temporarily, but it doesn't remove. It can even make it worse in some cases.

mYTH: "WD-40 removes scratches"

TRUTH: It only temporarily masks with oil. It is not a permanent solution.

mYTH: "Any scratch can be removed"

TRUTH: Only scratches in the clear coat can be removed. Deep scratches need to be touched up.

tRUTH: "Polishing removes clear coat"

Yes, that's why you can't polish endlessly. Clear coat is 40-60 microns, each polish removes 1-3 microns.

Conclusion

Most surface scratches can be removed with the right products and technique. The key is to correctly assess the depth and use the appropriate product.
