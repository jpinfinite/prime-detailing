Ceramic Coating vs PPF: Which to choose in 2025?

Complete comparison between ceramic coating and protective film. Costs, durability, protection and maintenance.

Understanding the differences

Ceramic Coating

Liquid based on nanotechnology (SiO2 or SiC) that creates a chemical bond with the paint, forming a protective layer of 2-5 microns.

How it works:

  • Applied as a liquid
  • Cures and hardens in 24-48 hours
  • Creates hydrophobic surface
  • Protects against chemicals and UV
  • Does not add significant thickness

PPF (Paint Protection Film)

Thermoplastic polyurethane film 150-200 microns thick, applied over paint.

How it works:

  • Applied as a physical film
  • Adheres with special adhesive
  • Absorbs physical impacts
  • Self-healing with heat
  • Adds physical layer of protection

Direct Comparison

Risk Protection

üèÜ Winner: PPF

  • Ceramic Coating: Light resistance to surface scratches (hardness 9H). Does NOT prevent scratches from stones, branches, etc.
  • PPF: Absorbs physical impacts. Protects against stones, branches, parking lot scratches. Film self-heals with heat.

Chemical protection

üèÜ Winner: Ceramic Coating

  • Ceramic Coating: Excellent against acids, alkalis, bird droppings, tree sap. Non-porous surface repels contaminants.
  • PPF: Good protection, but may yellow with prolonged exposure to UV and strong chemicals.

Appearance and gloss

winner: Ceramic Coating

  • Ceramic Coating: Enhances the paint's natural shine. Superior depth and clarity.
  • PPF: May have slight "orange peel". Premium PPFs are almost invisible, but never 100%.

Cost

üèÜ Winner: Ceramic Coating

Maintenance

  • PPF: Beware of aggressive products, can peel off at the edges, need to reapply coating

When to choose Ceramic Coating

choose ceramic coating if:

  • Your car is kept in a covered garage
  • You mainly drive in the city
  • You want to make washing and maintenance easier
  • You prioritize shine and appearance
  • You have a limited budget (R$1,500-4,000)
  • You want protection against chemicals and UV
  • Not too concerned about stones/scratches
  • Want to do it yourself (DIY possible)

Ideal profile: Enthusiast who takes good care of the car, washes it regularly, parks it in safe places.

When to choose PPF

choose PPF if:

  • You drive a lot on the highway
  • Your car is new or freshly painted
  • You want maximum protection against scratches
  • You park on the street frequently
  • You have a high budget (R$8,000-25,000)
  • High-value car you want to preserve
  • Region with a lot of stones/detritus
  • Plans to resell and wants to preserve value

Ideal profile: Premium/sports car owner who wants maximum protection and preservation of value.

The Best Solution: Combination

üí° Professional solution

Many detailers recommend: PPF on high-impact areas + Ceramic coating on the rest

Hybrid strategy

PPF on:

  • Full hood
  • Front bumper
  • Front fenders
  • Rear-view mirrors
  • Door handles
  • Behind the rear wheels

Ceramic coating on:

  • Roof
  • Doors
  • Rear cover
  • Rear bumper
  • Over the PPF (yes, you can!)

Total cost: R$4,000-8,000 (partial PPF + full coating)

Benefit: Maximum protection where you need it + economy + superior gloss

Myths and truths

‚ùå MYTH: "Ceramic coating protects against stones"

TRUTH: It doesn't. Coating is 2-5 microns. A stone at 100km/h passes through easily.

mYTH: "PPF leaves the car yellow"

TRUTH: Old PPFs do. Modern PPFs (post-2020) have UV protection and won't yellow for 10+ years.

‚ùå MYTH: "Ceramic coating lasts forever"

TRUTH: Nothing lasts forever. Consumer coatings last 2-5 years. Professional 5-10 years with maintenance.

tRUTH: "PPF can be removed without damage"

Yes, PPF can be removed completely without damaging paintwork (if applied correctly).

tRUTH: "You can apply ceramic coating over PPF"

Yes, and it's recommended! Coating over PPF improves hydrophobicity and facilitates cleaning.

Final decision

Choose CERAMIC COATING if:

  • Budget: R$ 1,500-4,000
  • Use: Urban, covered garage
  • Priority: Shine and ease of cleaning

Choose PPF COMPLETE if:

  • Budget: R$ 15,000-25,000
  • Use: Frequent highways
  • Priority: Maximum physical protection

Choose HYBRID (partial PPF + Coating) if:

  • Budget: R$ 4,000-8,000
  • Use: Mixed (city + highway)
  • Priority: Best value for money

Conclusion

There is no single answer. The choice depends on your budget, vehicle use and priorities. For most people, we recommend the hybrid solution: PPF on critical areas + ceramic coating on the rest.

This combination offers 80% of the protection of a full PPF for 40% of the cost, while maintaining the superior gloss of the ceramic coating.

Whatever you choose, any protection is better than none. Your car will thank you!
